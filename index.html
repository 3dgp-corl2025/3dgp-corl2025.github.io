Abstract

Recent advances in robotic manipulation have highlighted the effectiveness of learning from demonstration. However, while end-to-end policies excel in expressivity and flexibility, they struggle with generalization. An alternative approach of object-centric prediction offers improved generalizability but often relies on restrictive assumptions regarding object rigidity and unimodal scene placements. In this work, we introduce a hierarchical goal-prediction framework for object placement that overcomes these limitations. Specifically, we model global scene-level placements through a novel Dense Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM); we model local object-level configuration through a disentangled reference diffusion and shape diffusion. By decoupling placement prediction into these two stages, our method generalizes effectively to scene multi-modality while supporting high-precision and non-rigid placements. We validate our approach across a suite of challenging tasks, demonstrating strong performance in both rigid and non-rigid settings in simulation and in the real world.

Model Architecture

Global Placement Initialization

Our model initializes a rough global position using a novel dense GMM-based prediction module, a framework that models highly multi-modal placement distributions at the scene-level.

Local Configuration Refinement

Our model then proceeds with local configuration refinement, in which we introduce a disengantled shape diffusion and reference frame diffusion module along with several novel design choices that simultaneously allow precise and dense (i.e. non-rigid) transformations.
